Dropped in Google Rankings? Here’s How to Get Back to Page One

22 Jul 2025 · 6 min read

If your auto repair shop has suddenly dropped in Google rankings, it can feel like someone hit the brakes on your business — fewer calls, less traffic, and customers going to your competitors. The truth is, ranking drops happen more often than you'd think. But slipping doesn't mean sinking when you know what to look for.

  • Ranking drops often come from changes on your website, shifts in Google's algorithm, or increased competition.
  • Common culprits include slow loading speeds, broken links, and missing content.
  • Tools like Google Search Console and Analytics can help you spot the problem fast.
  • Fix issues on the pages that bring in the most traffic first.

Why You Dropped in Google Rankings

Most ranking drops happen for one of three reasons: something changed on your website, Google updated how it ranks pages, or a competitor has stepped up their SEO and moved ahead of you. The key is finding out what changed and fixing it before it hurts your visibility for too long.

Check Your Website First

Before diving into tools and data, inspect your website manually. Look at your homepage and key service pages. Make sure your most important content is still live and that nothing has been accidentally removed or changed. Double-check your titles, meta descriptions, and ensure the site is still fast and mobile-friendly. Also check your Google Business Profile — incorrect or outdated information there can affect how you appear in local searches.

Common SEO Issues That Cause Drops

  • Broken or deleted pages
  • Missing or duplicated metadata
  • Slow page loading times
  • Poor mobile usability
  • Outdated or thin content
  • Google Business Profile changes
  • Inconsistent directory listings

Use These Tools to Diagnose the Problem

Google Search Console will alert you to any crawl or indexing issues. Google Analytics will show you which pages lost the most traffic. Tools like Ubersuggest and SEMrush let you track keyword changes and compare your performance with local competitors. If your site feels slow, run a test with Google PageSpeed Insights to check load times and mobile performance.

How to Recover and Climb Back

Once you've identified the issue, prioritise pages that were driving the most traffic and leads. Refresh the content with updated information and local keywords. Build or reclaim local citations. Earn fresh reviews on your Google Business Profile. And if a competitor has clearly outpaced you, analyse what they're doing differently — then do it better.
