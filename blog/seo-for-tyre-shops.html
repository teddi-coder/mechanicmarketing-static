7 SEO for Tyre Shops Strategies to Rank #1 on Google

19 Apr 2025 · 7 min read

Owning a tyre shop isn't just about having the best selection or the lowest prices. If people can't find you online, they're not walking through your doors. Most people search Google before deciding where to buy tyres or get a repair — if your shop isn't ranking, you're handing business to your competitors.

1. Use Schema Markup to Stand Out in Search Results

Schema markup helps Google understand your site better and makes your listing more eye-catching. Local business schema tells Google your shop's name, address, phone number, and hours. Product schema shows tyre brands, sizes, and prices right in search results. FAQ schema helps Google pull answers to common questions like "How long do tyres last?" Use Google's Structured Data Markup Helper to generate the code, then test it with Google's Rich Results Test.

2. Optimise for Voice Search

More people are using voice search to find businesses, especially on phones and smart assistants. Instead of typing "best tyre shop near me," they ask "Where's the best place to buy tyres nearby?" Use natural, question-based phrases throughout your content. Add an FAQ section that answers questions directly. Make sure your Google Business Profile includes your city, neighbourhood, and landmarks.

3. Use the Right Keywords

People aren't searching for "premium rubber traction providers." They're looking for "cheap tyres in [city]" or "best all-season tyres near me." Research real search terms with Google Keyword Planner and use them naturally throughout your pages. Each page should target one specific tyre type or service for maximum relevance.

4. Optimise Your Google Business Profile

Your Google Business Profile is often the first thing potential customers see. Keep your name, address, and phone number consistent everywhere. Post photos of your shop and stock regularly. Actively collect Google reviews — quantity and quality both matter. Use the Posts feature to share seasonal promotions and updates.

5. Build Local Citations

Citations are mentions of your business on other websites — directories, industry sites, local listings. Consistent NAP (name, address, phone) across citations helps Google trust your location data and rank you higher in local results. List your shop on Yellow Pages, True Local, and any local business directories.

6. Create Service-Specific Content

Write dedicated pages or blog posts for each tyre type, service, and brand you stock. "Pirelli tyres in [city]" or "Run-flat tyre repairs near me" are high-intent searches with less competition than generic terms. Answering these searches on your site builds authority and attracts customers who are ready to buy.

7. Get Reviews Consistently

Ask every satisfied customer for a Google review. Make it easy — a QR code card at the register linking directly to your review page is one of the most effective tools available. Aim for a steady flow of reviews rather than a burst, as Google rewards consistency.
