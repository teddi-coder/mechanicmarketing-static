7 Strategies for Marketing a Collision Repair Shop

29 Mar 2025 · 6 min read

Getting more customers for your collision repair shop doesn't have to be complicated or expensive. With the right strategies, you can increase visibility, build trust, and turn more local searches into genuine bookings.

1. Be the First Shop People See Online

Most customers start their search on Google by typing things like "best auto body shop near me." If you're not showing up at the top, you're losing business. Claim and optimise your Google Business Profile — fill in your address, contact details, and hours. Add photos of your shop and past repairs. Encourage happy customers to leave reviews before they leave. Improve your website's local SEO with keywords like "collision repair in [your city]" and ensure your site loads fast on mobile.

2. Use Social Media to Actually Get Bookings

Posting random car photos isn't enough — you need content that builds trust. Before-and-after photos showcase real results and grab attention. Keep captions short and clear: "From wrecked to brand new in 48 hours!" has more impact than a long-winded explanation. Use quick videos to answer common questions about repair time, insurance coverage, and post-accident steps. And respond fast to messages — most people requesting quotes will book if they get a quick reply.

3. Get Steady Referrals from Insurance Agents and Dealerships

Referrals from insurance agents and car dealerships can fill your workshop consistently. Build relationships with local agents by visiting in person, offering a referral arrangement, and staying top of mind with occasional updates. Dealerships often need a trusted repairer for customer referrals and warranty work — being on their shortlist can provide a steady stream of jobs.

4. Promote Value-Added Services

Offer free estimates, complimentary loan cars, or a courtesy clean with every repair. These small extras differentiate your shop from competitors and give customers a reason to choose you over a shop with similar pricing. Promote these offers prominently on your website and in your ads.

5. Leverage Positive Reviews as Social Proof

Display Google review snippets prominently on your website and in your Facebook ads. When someone is deciding between two body shops, seeing "127 five-star reviews" next to your name versus a competitor's blank profile is often the deciding factor. Make collecting reviews a systematic part of your handover process.

6. Run Targeted Ads for High-Intent Customers

Focus Google Ads on searches like "panel beater near me" or "smash repairs [suburb]" — these are people who need help now, not later. On Facebook, target local residents who've recently experienced life events associated with car damage. A small, well-targeted budget consistently outperforms a large poorly-targeted one.
