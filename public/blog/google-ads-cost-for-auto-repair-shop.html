Google Ads Cost for Auto Repair Shop: How Much Should You Spend?

24 Jul 2025 · 6 min read

If you're an Aussie auto repair shop owner wondering about Google Ads cost and whether it's worth the spend, you're not alone. When done right, Google Ads can bring in jobs fast. But when your budget disappears without results, it's frustrating and costly.

  • Most independent Australian auto repair shops spend between $500–$3,000/month on Google Ads.
  • Cost per click (CPC) ranges from $2 to $10, depending on your location and service type.
  • Factors like competition, ad quality, and landing page experience affect how much you pay.
  • You should aim for a cost per lead (phone call or booking) under $50.
  • You don't need a huge budget to compete, but you do need to spend smartly.

What's a Realistic Google Ads Budget?

Most independent Australian mechanics and auto repair shops spend between $500 and $3,000 per month. For small shops in low-competition suburbs, $500–$800/month can bring in a steady stream of jobs. Larger shops or those in competitive areas like Sydney or Melbourne may need $1,500–$2,000/month to stay visible.

If you're just starting, test with $500–$1,000 for the first month. See how many leads it brings. If you're booking jobs and making a profit, scale up gradually.

What Impacts the Cost of Google Ads?

The cost of Google Ads isn't fixed. Your location plays a significant role — if you're in a busy city, you'll compete with more shops, which drives up the cost per click. The services you promote also matter. Ads for "rego inspections" or "emergency towing" might cost more than general "car servicing."

Your ad quality score — which includes relevance, landing page quality, and click-through rate — affects your cost. Better ads cost less to show.

How Much Does It Cost Per Enquiry?

A mechanic in Geelong spending around $600/month targeting brake and logbook services, averaging $6 per click with a 15% conversion rate, gets 100 clicks and 15 leads — roughly $40 per lead. A busier shop in Sydney's Inner West spending $1,800/month on high-value services like engine diagnostics and Euro car servicing, with better optimisation, can achieve a similar or lower cost per lead.

Common Mistakes That Blow Your Budget

  • Targeting the wrong keywords — "mechanic" is too broad. Use "logbook service Southport" instead.
  • Sending traffic to your homepage instead of a service-specific landing page
  • Not tracking conversions — if you can't measure it, you can't improve it
  • Setting and forgetting — Google Ads needs regular optimisation

DIY vs Agency

DIY is cheaper upfront but can lead to costly mistakes — wasted spend, poor targeting, and missed conversions. A good agency adds value through better strategy, structure, and ongoing optimisation. The key is finding one that specialises in your industry and tracks results transparently.
