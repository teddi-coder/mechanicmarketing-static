3 Scalable Marketing Tactics for Growing Auto Repair Chains

10 Aug 2025 · 8 min read

Growing from a single auto repair shop to multiple locations is a significant step. What worked when you were a one-shop operation may not work across several sites. The key is finding marketing tactics that are repeatable, trackable, and flexible enough to adapt to each new location without reinventing the wheel every time.

  • Multi-location shops need consistent branding but localised visibility.
  • Utilise location-specific SEO pages to optimise each shop's ranking.
  • Centralised ad campaigns allow better budget control and performance tracking.
  • Automated email follow-ups drive repeat business without extra admin.
  • Measure by cost per lead and store-level performance, not just clicks.

Why Scaling Marketing Gets Harder as You Grow

When running multiple shops, you're suddenly dealing with different suburbs, search terms, and demographics; various sets of reviews and business listings; ads that need to speak to local audiences but follow central branding; and staff at each site who may handle leads differently. That's why scalability matters — you need marketing that grows with you, not against you.

Tactic 1: Local SEO at Scale

Each location should have its dedicated landing page optimised for local search. Pages like "Logbook Servicing in Newcastle | [Your Brand]" should include local keywords, photos from the actual location, reviews, and a map. Also create and optimise a Google Business Profile for each site with real photos, opening hours, and reviews collected separately for each shop.

Tactic 2: Paid Ads With Centralised Strategy

You don't need a separate Google Ads account for every location. Run multi-location campaigns from a single account by segmenting campaigns or ad groups by suburb or store. This gives you control over the message, budget, and reporting while still targeting each local area with location-specific landing pages.

Facebook and Instagram ads can also be managed from a single Business Manager, allowing you to A/B test creative across stores and push promotions by postcode.

Tactic 3: Automate Email Follow-Ups

Use an automated system that connects to your booking software and sends personalised follow-up emails or SMS messages after service. Messages like "Thanks for booking with us in Newcastle — your next logbook service is due in 6 months" keep your customer experience consistent while saving hours of admin across every location.

How to Measure What's Working

Don't just track clicks or impressions. Track how many calls or bookings each tactic is generating for each shop. Use call tracking numbers on each landing page and link Google Ads to Google Analytics for precise data on which locations are getting results and which need a tweak.
